Architecture of Candi Penataran

As previously mentioned, Penaratan Temple is the widest and grandest temple in East Java. This temple has a total area of 12,946 m2. Inside, there are various buildings located from the northwest to the southeast of the temple complex. At the back of the main temple there is a river that has its headwaters on Mount Kelud. In front of the main temple, there are also several perwara temples and a hall.

Main Gate

On the front, you can see two statues named Arca Dwarapala. In addition, there are remnants of the gate, terrace hall, bale agung and the Angka Tahun Temple. The following is an explanation of the architecture in the front area.

Arca Dwarapala

These two statues are statues flanking the gate so they are also known as door guards. Local people call this Dwarapala statue as Reco Pentung. Here you can see the carved numbers that read 1242 Saka or 1320 AD.

Bale Agung

You can see Bale Agung in the front yard area of Penataran Temple. Bale Agung is located jutting forward on the northwest side of the front yard. The entire Bale Agung is made of stone with plain walls and two steps to the southeast. This building is used as a means of deliberation for priests. This building has an area of 37 m x 18.84 m x 1.44 m.

Pendopo Terrace

You can see the Pendopo terrace which is located to the southeast of Bale Agung. The building is rectangular in shape with an area of 29.05 m x 9.22 m x 1.5 m. This place is said to have a function as a place to put offerings in religious ceremonies. This pavilion is also commonly used as a resting place for kings and other royal nobles. Like Bale Agung, Pendopo Terrace is also wrapped in a snake terrace where the head is poking out between the pillars of the building. You can also see some reliefs on the walls of this terrace. These reliefs tell the story of Bubhuksah and Handle Aking, Sri Tanjung and Sang Setyawan.

Main Temple

In this section, you can see three terraces that are 7.19 meters high each. In the main temple area, there is a Mahakala statue on the side of the stairs. Here you can see the carving of the year numbers showing the year 1269 Saka or 1347 AD. On the first terrace, you can see the reliefs of the Ramayana story depicted around the walls of the main temple. To read this relief, you must follow the direction of Prasawijaya starting from the other west side. Furthermore, on the second terrace there is a relief in the form of the story of Krnayana. In contrast to the reliefs of the Ramayana, this relief is read in a pradaksina way, which is read clockwise. Finally, on the third terrace you can see reliefs with images of dragons and winged lions. This third terrace has a square shape.

Temple Number Year

From the terrace pavilion, now we go to Angka Tahum Temple. This temple has other names such as Brawijaya Temple and Ganesha Temple. This temple can be called as one of the most famous buildings in the Penataran Temple area. In this temple is written the number 1291 Saka or 1369 AD. Angka Tahun Temple has an entrance to the northwest. Inside the Angka Tahun temple, you can see a statue of Ganesha made of stone. This Ganesha statue is made sitting on the padmasana. In this temple, you can also see the Surya Majapahit relief which is located on the stone covering the temple cupola.

Naga Temple

It's named Candi Naga because the architecture of the body has dragons wrapped around this temple. Here, you can also see the props in the form of figures dressed as kings, which are located in the corner of the building, in the middle of the wall and to the left and right of the entrance. The reliefs in Naga Temple are a type of relief made with medallion motifs. This Naga Temple has an area of 6.57 m x 4.83 m x 4.7 m.

Penataran Temple

Penataran Temple Tour Can Be The Main Tourist Destination

Blitar - The East Java Provincial Government asked the artists and the local government to work together, so that Penataran Temple tourism could become the main destination for tourists seeking artistic and cultural entertainment in Blitar Regency.

Penataran Temple, The Largest Hindu Temple In Blitar

Penataran Temple is an ancient site as well as the main tourist attraction in Blitar district besides the Bung Karno Tomb. This temple site with a Hindu religious background is the largest temple site in Blitar which has existed since the days of the Kediri kingdom and was still used until the time of the Majapahit kingdom.

Penataran Temple, Mainstay of Archaeological Tourism in East Java

The Nglegok sub-district, Blitar district, has tourism potential which until now has become a mainstay in East Java. This temple, which is located in Penataran Village, is the largest temple in East Java.
